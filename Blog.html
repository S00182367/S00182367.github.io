WEEK ONE 26th April – 3rd May

I began the week by coming up with a concept for the game I wished to make for my Work Placement Project. I had three ideas in mind at the time which where a Platform game, a Kart Racing game or a Yugioh type card game. I decided to do a Platform game in the end. I did not really know what I wanted to do, did I want a 3D Platform or 2D Platform game. I would have loved to make a 3D game, but I decided to do a 2D game as it would be more likely what I would be able to achieve on my own and withing the time limit. It would also allow me to add more of the features that I wanted for the game.

WEEK TWO 3rd May – 10th May

I began this week with the weekly meeting with Neil. We outlined our ideas for our games and many people has very interesting ideas. I decided to do my work placement project on my own and it would be a 2D Platform game. After the meeting I spent the week trying out how to best develop the game and what engine in which to use. I tried out using Unreal Engine because I really liked the Blueprint type of coding, but I discovered that trying to create a 2D game in Unreal is really made far too complicated, so I tried out Unity and found it was far better for developing 2D games. My choice was to sacrifice blueprint coding for an easier development experience or chose blueprint coding and have a hard job with general development. In the end I decided that I would use Unity for this Project.

WEEK SEVEN 7th June – 14th June

This week I did research on how to program the camera and I also l learned about using Parallax scrolling in 2D video games. Parallax scrolling is where the background images move past the camara more slowly than the foreground, creating an illusion of depth in a 2D game. I introduced this into my test level by using free background assets and tested to make sure that it was working properly. I also worked on the camera to make sure that it follows the Player throughout the test level.

WEEK EIGHT 14th June – 21st June

The vertical camera worked in much the same way as the Horizontal camera except in this case it followed the Player as it jumped up. It also followed the Player as it ascended to higher platforms in the test level.
